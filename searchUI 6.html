This website demonstrates a method for representing atoms and molecules as integers.

This website was built, and is updated, by Michael E. Campos. See bottom of page for contact information.

Instructions

1. Find an atom in the table of elements.

2. Click the atom to input 1 of that atom.

3. Repeat steps 1 and 2 until all atoms are inputed.

4. Click "SEARCH" to search for molecules composed of the inputed atoms.

Explanation of the Output

The output is a list of all possible moleculecular formulas that can be created using the inputed atoms. For example, if 100 carbon, 100 hydrogen, and 100 oxygen atoms are inputed, the output will list many molecular formula variations of CHO, HO, CH, etc. However, the output will not list variations with more than 100 C, H, or O atoms atom.
